## 2. What to SAY / what NOT to say

### ✅ DO say (approved, copy freely)
- "5g creatine + 5g collagen + a complete multivitamin. One scoop."
- "The studied dose" / "the studied amount is 3–5g a day — this is the full 5g."
- Creatine claim, exactly: "increases physical performance in successive bursts of short-term, high-intensity exercise."
- "Biotin contributes to the maintenance of normal hair." / "Vitamin C contributes to normal collagen formation."
- "Halal certified — HMC protocol GB18/2026, including source-of-slaughter certification for the bovine collagen."
- "Third-party batch tested — certificate of analysis per lot." / "Made in a GMP-certified UK facility."
- "First week is a half scoop — the gentle way to start."
- "From £1.62 a day, subscribed." / "30-day flavour guarantee — hate it, they refund the pouch."
- "20% of profits, audited, fund refuge, care and crisis support — and you choose the cause in the app."
- Dandelion / cranberry: **only** "traditionally used for…" phrasing.
- Inositol: **at most** "myo + D-chiro inositol 40:1 — the ratio studied in PCOS research."

### ❌ DON'T say (any of these voids the deliverable — we cannot pay for unusable content)
- Anything as a **customer testimonial**: "I've been taking it for X weeks", "my results", "it changed my skin/hair/energy", star ratings, "verified buyer". You are presenting facts as a creator, not reviewing as a customer. (UK DMCC Act 2024 — fake/unverifiable reviews are a banned practice.)
- Results promises or timelines: "you'll see/feel X by week Y", before/after, transformation framing.
- Disease/treatment words: cure, treat, prevent, fix. No hormone-balancing promises, fertility, mental-health, or weight-loss claims. No "helps PCOS".
- "Clinically proven" about the product. (Ingredients are studied; the product is not "proven".)
- "No bloating" or any zero-side-effect promise.
- "Doctor recommended", medical settings, white coats.
- Problem-calling the viewer: don't open with "struggling with…?", "tired of feeling…?"
- Urgency/scarcity: "selling fast", countdowns, "only X left".
- Diet-culture language: detox, cleanse, weight loss, "glow-up", "water weight".
- Naming specific charity partners (not yet signed) — say "vetted causes". Never use recipients' hardship as content.
- Health claims for the *product* beyond the approved lines above.

**Must appear in your caption:** `#ad` (first hashtag) + "For healthy adults. Not suitable if pregnant or breastfeeding." (The brand adds the full food-supplement statement on landing pages.)
