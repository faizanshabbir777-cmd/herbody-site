# HerBody — Rejected Claims (Banned List)

These are **automatic rejections**. Any draft containing one (or a paraphrase of one) fails the compliance gate regardless of hedging, "may", "could", asterisks or citations. This list is loaded into every agent's system prompt and checked again by the human reviewer.

| # | Banned claim / practice | Why it's banned |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **"Balances your hormones" / any hormone-fixing outcome promise** ("hormone support", "regulates your cycle", "fixes hormonal chaos") | No authorised claim supports a hormone *outcome*. Implies treatment of a physiological condition → medicinal claim territory (ASA/CAP §12, MHRA borderline). The single verbatim B6 register claim (see Claims Matrix row 11) is not a licence for any paraphrase. |
| 2 | **"De-bloat" as an efficacy promise** ("beats bloating", "flat-tummy formula", "bye bye bloat") | Dandelion has no authorised GB claim; bloating framing implies treating a symptom. Also violates brand hard rule 7 — no second-person problem-calling ("Bloated?"). Traditional-use framing only, per Claims Matrix row 8. |
| 3 | **Skin-transformation promises** ("glowing skin in 4 weeks", "erases fine lines", "younger-looking skin", "glass skin") | No authorised claim for collagen or hyaluronic acid exists. Only the exact vitamin C and biotin register claims are permitted. Transformation language is also a beauty-efficacy claim requiring substantiation we do not hold. |
| 4 | **"Clinically proven" applied to the product** ("clinically proven formula", "proven results") | Ingredients (Creapure®, Verisol®) appear in studies; **the finished product has never been trialled**. Claiming product-level proof is misleading (CAP 3.1) and a brand hard rule. Ceiling: "studied ingredients at studied doses." |
| 5 | **Fake or unverifiable reviews, testimonials, star ratings, "verified buyer" tags, invented usage tenure** ("I've taken it for 3 months and…") — including UGC scripted to *sound* like a customer | **Banned practice under the DMCC Act 2024**, CMA-enforceable with fines up to 10% of global turnover. HerBody has zero customers and zero reviews. Reviews module is disabled (Decision D6). Creator content must be labelled as creator/brand content. |
| 6 | **Fake urgency or scarcity** (countdown timers, "only 7 left", "X people viewing", "offer ends tonight" when it doesn't) | DMCC Act 2024 banned/blacklisted pressure practices + CAP 3.31. Brand hard rule 4. If a claim about availability isn't literally, verifiably true, it doesn't run — and even true scarcity is off-brand. |
| 7 | **Before/after imagery or narrative** (split-screen body/skin comparisons, "week 1 vs week 8") | Implies product-level transformation results we cannot substantiate; body-transformation imagery violates the brand's photography direction and ASA social-responsibility rules on body image. |
| 8 | **Weight-loss claims of any kind** ("lose weight", "water weight", "fat burner", "supports weight management", styling the product next to scales/tape measures) | No authorised claim; diet-culture framing violates brand voice ("no shrink-and-pink"); weight-control claims for this composition are unsubstantiated and high ASA-risk for women's-wellness advertisers. |
| 9 | **"Doctor recommended" / "GP approved" / white-coat imagery implying medical endorsement** | CAP 12.18 restricts health-professional endorsement for health products; we hold no such endorsement. Also fabricating one would compound into rule 5 territory. |
| 10 | **Disease / treatment / prevention claims** ("prevents UTIs", "helps PCOS", "treats hair loss", "fights fatigue [as a condition]", anxiety/mood/mental-health claims, fertility claims) | Medicinal claims make the product an unlicensed medicine (MHRA) and breach CAP 12. "Studied in PCOS research" (informational surfaces only) is the absolute ceiling — see Claims Matrix row 4. |
| 11 | **Guaranteed results** ("results guaranteed or your money back", "works for everyone") | Unsubstantiable efficacy guarantee. Note: the **30-day flavour guarantee is fine** — it guarantees a refund if you dislike the taste, and must never be reworded to imply a results guarantee. |
| 12 | **Charity inflation or trauma-bait** (naming unsigned partners, "we've donated £X" pre-audit, imagery/copy using women's hardship as ad bait) | Pledge is "20% of profits, audited annually" — nothing more until the audit exists and partners sign. Brand hard rule 6: solidarity not saviour. |
| 13 | **"Detox", "cleanse", "flush toxins"** | Meaningless-and-misleading category claims (ASA has ruled against them repeatedly); lexicon-banned in `brand-voice.md`. |
| 14 | **US regulatory boilerplate** (DSHEA disclaimer, "FDA registered facility") | Wrong jurisdiction; confusing and potentially misleading to UK consumers. This build is ASA/CAP + GB NHC only (Decision D7). |

**Paraphrase rule:** the gate rejects on meaning, not string match. "Feel less puffy" = rule 2. "Your skin will thank you in a month" = rule 3. "Real users love it" with no real users = rule 5.

**Escalation:** if a draft seems borderline, it is NEEDS_REVIEW, never PASS. The founder rejects by default; `docs/CLAIMS_MATRIX.md` gains a row only after a fresh GB NHC register lookup confirms a basis.
